1) Description of Measurement

CCI measures the joint space between the occipital condyles and the lateral masses of C1. It is currently considered the most reliable CT-based metric for diagnosing occipitoatlantal dissociation.

2) Instructions to Measure

  • Use coronal CT reconstructions.
  • Measure the distance between each occipital condyle and the corresponding C1 superior articular surface.
  • Record the maximum value or the mean, depending on institutional protocol.

    • 3) Normal vs. Pathologic Ranges

  • Normal: ≤2 mm
  • 2 mm widening is diagnostic of occipital-C1 dissociation, even in the absence of gross displacement.
  • In children, a "medial occipital condyle notch" is common (peak ages 2-4) and a normal developmental irregularity. It should not be included in the CCI measurement, as it would falsely increase the value.
  • Normal: The joint space is typically < 1.5 mm on CT.
  • Pathologic (Adults):
  • > 2.0 mm: This is the widely accepted cutoff for diagnosing AOD in adults.
  • > 1.5 mm: Some studies advocate this stricter cutoff to increase sensitivity,

    5) Other info....

    In pediatric groups, the CCI shows 100% sensitivity and specificity when used correctly, unlike the Rule of 12 (BDI/BAI), which has a sensitivity of 31-50%. This is because the BDI depends on dens (ossification) and the BAI on the basion (shape), whereas the CCI directly measures the injury site.
