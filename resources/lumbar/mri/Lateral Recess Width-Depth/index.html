1) Description of Measurement

Lateral recess width (also termed lateral recess depth) quantifies the available space for the traversing nerve root between the posterior vertebral body/disc complex and the superior articular facet. This is the most common site of nerve root compression in degenerative lumbar spinal stenosis and is particularly sensitive to facet hypertrophy, ligamentum flavum thickening, and disc bulge.

This measurement directly reflects the risk of radiculopathy and complements central canal CSA in evaluating symptomatic stenosis.

2) Instructions to Measure

  • Use the sagittal MRI to identify the lumbar level where symptoms or stenosis are most pronounced.
  • Scroll to the corresponding axial T2-weighted slice at the level of the pedicle and disc.
  • Identify:
  • The medial border of the superior articular facet (posterior boundary).
  • The posterolateral margin of the vertebral body or disc (anterior boundary).
  • Draw a straight line between these two points, oriented along the expected path of the traversing nerve root.
  • Measure the distance in millimeters (mm) — this is the lateral recess width/depth.

    • 3) Normal vs. Pathologic Ranges

  • Normal: ≥ 5 mm
  • Relative Stenosis: 3 - 5 mm
  • Severe lateral recess stenosis: < 3 mm
  • Critical stenosis: Visible root impingement/obliteration

    • Key points:

  • Values < 3 mm strongly correlate with symptomatic radiculopathy.
  • The side with the smallest width should be documented.

    5) Other info....

    Lateral recess stenosis is often the primary driver of unilateral leg symptoms.

    Should be evaluated with:

  • Dural sac CSA
  • Foraminal area

    • Central canal diameter may appear preserved even when lateral recess width is critically narrowed; therefore this measurement is essential in symptomatic patients.
