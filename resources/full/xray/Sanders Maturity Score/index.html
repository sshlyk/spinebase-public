1) Description of Measurement

The Sanders Maturity Score is a hand radiograph–based skeletal maturity system developed to more precisely estimate remaining growth potential in children and adolescents, particularly those with scoliosis. It evaluates the pattern of epiphyseal and physeal maturation in the hand and wrist and directly tracks progression through the peak height velocity (PHV) period.

2) Instructions to Measure

  • Obtain a standard PA radiograph of the left hand and wrist.
  • Evaluate the morphology and closure status of:
  • Phalangeal epiphyses
  • Metacarpal epiphyses
  • Distal radius epiphysis
  • Assign a Sanders stage (1–8) based on specific epiphyseal shape changes, capping, and fusion patterns (please see the image).
  • Staging follows a sequential maturation pathway, moving from open physes with minimal epiphyseal enlargement to complete physeal closure.

    • 3) Normal vs. Pathologic Ranges

    Sanders stages represent normal skeletal development phases. Their importance lies in growth-risk stratification.

  • Stages 1–2:
  • Prepubertal growth
  • Low immediate progression risk
  • Stages 3–4:
  • Early puberty and onset of peak height velocity
  • Highest risk of scoliosis progression
  • Stage 5:
  • Late peak height velocity
  • Growth rate slowing, but progression risk persists
  • Stages 6–7:
  • Decelerating growth, approaching maturity
  • Reduced progression risk
  • Stage 8:
  • Complete skeletal maturity
  • Minimal to no risk of curve progression

    5) Other info....

    The Sanders Maturity Score is valuable as it indicates peak height velocity, the best predictor of rapid scoliosis progression. The Risser sign, reflecting later skeletal maturity, may underestimate growth potential early in puberty.
