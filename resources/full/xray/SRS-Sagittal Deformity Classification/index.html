1) Description of Measurement

The SRS-Sagittal Deformity Classification was developed by the Scoliosis Research Society Adult Spinal Deformity Committee to provide a reliable radiographic taxonomy for adult spinal deformity (ASD) that incorporates not only coronal curve patterns but also regional sagittal malalignment, lumbar degenerative pathology, and global balance.

The system includes:

  • Six primary coronal curve types
  • Regional sagittal modifiers (PT, MT, TL, L) when outside normal limits
  • Lumbar degenerative modifiers (disc degeneration, listhesis, junctional curves)
  • Global balance modifiers (sagittal or coronal imbalance)

    • This framework was the direct conceptual precursor to the modern SRS-Schwab classification, emphasizing the importance of sagittal alignment and degenerative features in adult deformity.

    2) Instructions to Measure

    Obtain standing long-cassette AP and lateral full-length radiographs

  • Determine Primary Curve Type

    • Six Major curve patterns:

  • ST: Single thoracic
  • DT: Double thoracic
  • DM: Double major
  • TM: Triple major
  • TL: Thoracolumbar
  • L: Lumbar (‘de novo’ or idiopathic)
  • SP: Primary sagittal plane deformity (no major coronal curve)

    • SRS Definitions of Regions

  • Thoracic: apex T2-T11/12 disc
  • Thoracolumbar: apex T12-L1
  • Lumbar: apex L1-L2 disc to L4

    • Criteria for major curves

  • Thoracic: Cobb ≥ 40° AND apical vertebra lateral to C7 plumbline
  • Thoracolumbar/Lumbar: Cobb ≥ 30° AND apical vertebra lateral to CSVL
  • Primary sagittal deformity: no major coronal curve + ≥ 1 sagittal modifier outside normal range

    •  

  • Regional Sagittal Modifiers (only if abnormal)

    • Measure sagittal Cobb angles on lateral films:

  • PT: T2-T5, abnormal if > 20°
  • MT: T5-T12, abnormal if > 50°
  • TL: T10-L2, abnormal if > 20°
  • L: T12-S1, abnormal if > 40°

    •  

  • Lumbar Degenerative Modifiers (if present)

    • Apply if radiographic evidence of:

  • DDD: disc height loss + facet arthropathy (record lowest level L1-S1)
  • LIS: rotational, lateral, antero- or retrolisthesis ≥ 3 mm (record lowest level L1-L5)
  • JCT: L5-S1 junctional curve ≥ 10° (intersection of superior endplates of L5 and S1)

    •  

  • Global Balance Modifiers (if present)
  • SB (sagittal balance): C7 plumbline ≥ 5 cm anterior or posterior to sacral promontory
  • CB (coronal balance): C7 plumbline ≥ 3 cm right or left of CSVL

    • 3) Normal vs. Pathologic Ranges

  • Thoracic Cobb (adult major): < 40° (normal), ≥ 40° (pathologic)
  • TL/L Cobb (adult major): < 30° (normal), ≥ 30° (pathologic)
  • PT kyphosis: ≤ 20° (normal), > 20° (pathologic)
  • MT kyphosis: ≤ 50° (normal), > 50° (pathologic)
  • TL kyphosis: ≤ 20° (normal), > 20° (pathologic)
  • Lumbar kyphosis: ≤ 40° (normal), > 40° (pathologic)
  • Listhesis: < 3 mm (normal), ≥ 3 mm (pathologic)
  • Sagittal balance: < 5 cm (normal), ≥ 5 cm (pathologic)
  • Coronal balance: < 3 cm (normal), ≥ 3 cm (pathologic)

    5) Other info....

    The SRS-Sagittal Deformity Classification system highlights that adult deformity is dominated:

  • Lumbar degenerative disease,
  • Loss of sagittal alignment, and
  • Global imbalance, features largely absent in AIS.

    • This classification directly influenced development of the SRS-Schwab system, which later incorporated pelvic parameters (PI–LL mismatch, SVA, PT) for stronger correlation with disability and outcomes.
